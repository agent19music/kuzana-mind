# Messaging Hierarchy

The single source of truth for how we talk about Kuzana Brain — ordered from most important to most detailed. Use higher-level messages when time is short. Drill down when you have more space or a more informed audience.

---

## Level 1 — The Problem (5 seconds)

> "Your team's knowledge is trapped. In docs no one can find. In heads no one has time to pick."

Use this: cold outreach subject lines, event intros, the first sentence of any LinkedIn post.

---

## Level 2 — The Product (15 seconds)

> "Kuzana Brain is an AI that answers your team's questions — from your existing Google Docs. If the docs don't cover it, it tells you exactly who on your team to ask. It never makes an answer up."

Use this: elevator pitch, demo cold open, email preview text, Twitter/X bio.

---

## Level 3 — Why It's Different (60 seconds)

Three things no other tool does together:

**1. It uses your docs — not a new knowledge base.**
You already have Google Docs. We connect to those. No migration, no rebuilding, no IT project. Ingest in minutes.

**2. It never hallucinates.**
If the answer isn't in your docs, Kuzana doesn't guess. It says: "I don't have this documented — here's who to ask," and surfaces the right person's name, title, and email.

**3. It's built for how African professional teams actually work.**
Org structures are flatter. Knowledge lives in WhatsApp threads and personal Google Drives. People wear multiple hats. Kuzana's staff directory routing understands that "who handles procurement" isn't always in the org chart.

Use this: first 60 seconds of a demo, landing page body copy, one-pager body.

---

## Level 4 — Proof Points

Use specific, verifiable behaviours as proof. Not claims — demonstrations.

| Claim | Proof behaviour to show |
|---|---|
| "Answers from your docs" | Type "what is the leave policy?" → get the exact policy chunk, with source doc cited |
| "Never hallucinates" | Type an obscure question → get "I don't have this — ask Amara Osei (Chief People Officer)" instead of a made-up answer |
| "Routes to the right person" | Staff directory shows name, title, department, email — not just "contact HR" |
| "Fast to set up" | POST /ingest runs in under 60 seconds on 7 docs |
| "Works on existing docs" | Demo uses real markdown files, same structure as Google Docs export |

Use this: demo walkthrough, case study proof section, sales objection handling.

---

## Level 5 — Objection Handling

**"We already have Notion / Confluence."**
Those are writing tools. Kuzana is a retrieval tool. You still need somewhere to write the docs — Kuzana makes them answerable. The two aren't in competition.

**"Can't people just use ChatGPT?"**
ChatGPT doesn't know your company. It knows the internet. It will confidently answer "what's our leave policy?" with a wrong answer because it has no idea what your policy says. Kuzana only answers from *your* docs.

**"We're too small for this."**
You're too small *not* to have this. At 15 people, one new hire asking three questions a day already interrupts five senior people. That compounds fast.

**"What if our docs are messy?"**
Good. Kuzana surfaces what's there and flags gaps. When it can't find an answer, it routes to a person — which tells you exactly which topics need better documentation.

**"Is our data secure?"**
Your documents are processed through your own Google Drive credentials and stored in your own database. Nothing is shared with third parties. You control the data.

## Audience-Specific Messaging

### Operations Manager
**Lead with:** time saved. "Stop being the human search engine for your team."
**Key message:** "When someone on your team asks a question you've answered 10 times before, Kuzana answers it for you."
**Proof:** Staff directory routing + source citation shows they won't be blindsided by wrong answers.

### HR / People Lead
**Lead with:** onboarding friction. "New hires shouldn't need a buddy to find the leave policy."
**Key message:** "Everything you've ever documented — policies, processes, contacts — becomes answerable on day one."
**Proof:** Show the onboarding process doc being retrieved instantly.

### Founder / COO
**Lead with:** scale. "As you grow, knowledge stops travelling as easily."
**Key message:** "Kuzana is the layer that makes your institutional knowledge accessible without you in the room."
**Proof:** Show the org structure doc + staff directory working together.

### Investor
**Lead with:** category + timing. "Knowledge management is broken for African teams, and AI makes it solvable for the first time."
**Key message:** "We're building the internal knowledge layer for Sub-Saharan Africa's 10M+ professional SMEs — starting with a no-hallucination AI assistant that plugs into Google Workspace."
**Proof:** Working demo, real embeddings pipeline, clear production path via service account.

## Messages That Are Off-Brand

Avoid these — they don't fit Kuzana's voice or positioning:

| Off-brand | Why | On-brand alternative |
|---|---|---|
| "Leverage AI to empower your team" | Filler. Means nothing. | "Ask a question. Get an answer." |
| "Seamless integration" | Every product says this. | "Connects to your Google Docs in minutes." |
| "Cutting-edge AI" | Vague. Sounds like a press release. | "Built on Gemini embeddings and pgvector — same tech powering enterprise search." |
| "All-in-one knowledge platform" | Invites comparison to Notion/Confluence. | "Not a new tool. A smarter way into the tools you already use." |
| "For teams of all sizes" | ICP-avoidant. | "Built for 10–200 person teams in Africa." |
