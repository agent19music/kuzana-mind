How Kuzana Brain sits relative to alternatives — used for sales conversations, investor decks, and positioning decisions.

## The Competitive Landscape

Buyers aren't comparing Kuzana to one product — they're comparing it to their current behaviour. The real alternatives are:

1. **Asking a colleague directly** (the status quo)
2. **Searching Google Drive manually**
3. **Using ChatGPT and hoping it's right**
4. **Buying a knowledge management tool** (Notion, Confluence, Guru)

Kuzana competes with all four simultaneously, and beats each for a different reason.

## Detailed Competitor Profiles

### ChatGPT / Claude (ad hoc use)
**What people use it for:** Quick answers, writing help, explaining concepts.
**Why it fails for internal knowledge:** It has no idea what your company's policies, processes, or people look like. It will answer a question about your leave policy with generic advice and sound confident doing it. That's worse than not answering.
**Kuzana's edge:** We only answer from *your* docs. If we don't have it, we say so and route to a person.

---

### Notion AI
**What it's good at:** Writing, organising, structuring information inside Notion.
**Why it fails here:** You have to build the Notion wiki first. Most African SMEs don't have a Notion wiki — they have Google Docs. Notion AI doesn't read your Drive.
**Kuzana's edge:** Works on what you already have. No migration, no new writing tool, no IT project.

---

### Confluence
**What it's good at:** Large engineering teams that need structured documentation and version history.
**Why it fails here:** Enterprise product, enterprise complexity, enterprise price. Overkill for a 30-person ops team. Requires dedicated admin. Nobody searches it because it requires you to know the right keywords.
**Kuzana's edge:** Conversational — ask a natural language question, get a natural language answer with a source link.

---

### Guru
**What it's good at:** Customer-facing teams that need verified, up-to-date answers (sales, support).
**Why it fails here:** Another knowledge base to build and maintain. Requires someone to own the content. Not designed for internal ops/HR questions.
**Kuzana's edge:** No content management overhead — Kuzana re-ingests your existing docs on a schedule. No one has to write or update Guru cards.

---

### Google Drive Search
**What it's good at:** Finding a file you know exists.
**Why it fails here:** Keyword-only search. Can't answer "what is our policy on..." — only finds files that contain those words. Doesn't synthesise across documents. Doesn't tell you who to ask.
**Kuzana's edge:** Semantic search + synthesis + staff routing. Not just finding the file — answering the question.

## Positioning Decision: Where Kuzana Wins

We win when the buyer has:
- **Existing Google Docs** (not Notion, not Confluence) with at least some documentation
- **A team large enough that questions get lost** (10+ people)
- **No appetite for a long implementation** — they need this working this week, not in 6 months
- **A real cost from "go ask someone"** — usually ops, HR, or an overloaded founder

We lose (or should walk away) when:
- The company has no documentation at all — they need a documentation culture first
- They're locked into Microsoft 365 / SharePoint (different connector, post-MVP)
- They want a full HR system with workflows, approvals, and payroll integration

## The Category We're Creating

Kuzana isn't trying to win the "knowledge management software" category — that's dominated by Confluence and Notion, who have 10-year head starts.

We're creating (or claiming early leadership in) a new category:

**AI Knowledge Routing** — a system that doesn't just store knowledge, but actively retrieves and routes it, without hallucinating and without requiring a new knowledge base.

The pitch to analysts and investors: knowledge management tools got people to *write things down*. Kuzana makes written knowledge *findable and trustworthy*, specifically for markets underserved by Western enterprise SaaS.

## Battlecards (for Sales Conversations)

### "We already use Notion"
> "Great — Kuzana doesn't replace Notion. It makes your existing docs answerable through a chat interface. If you ever have docs in Google Drive too, those work as well. Think of it as search that actually understands questions, sitting on top of whatever you're already using."

### "We just use ChatGPT for this"
> "Try asking ChatGPT what your company's expense approval process is. It won't know — it'll make something up. Kuzana only answers from your actual company documents, cites the source, and tells you who to ask when the docs don't cover it."

### "This sounds like something we could build ourselves"
> "You could — and it would take an engineer 2–3 weeks to build a basic version, plus ongoing maintenance for the embedding pipeline and staff directory routing. Kuzana is that system, live in minutes, maintained for you."

### "We're not sure our team would use it"
> "What's the question your team asks most often that they should already know the answer to? We'll show you what Kuzana returns for that question in your documents, live, right now."
