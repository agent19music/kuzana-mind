# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Who we're building for, in enough detail to recognise them on sight — and know when someone isn't them.

---

## Primary ICP: The Ops-Burdened Leader

### Who they are
**Title:** Head of Operations, COO, HR Manager, Office Manager, or a founder who is also the de facto COO
**Company:** 15–80 person professional services firm, fintech, NGO, or fast-growing startup
**Location:** Lagos, Nairobi, Accra, Johannesburg (primary). Other SSA cities secondary.
**Reports to:** CEO / Founder

### Their day
They are the human router. When someone needs to know how to submit an expense, what the leave policy says, who owns client escalations, or what the onboarding process is — they get asked. They answer the same 20 questions on rotation. They've written the policy docs. Nobody reads them.

They are smart enough to know this is a process problem. They don't have time to fix it properly. They will pay for something that fixes it without creating more work for them.

### What they already use
- Google Workspace (Docs, Drive, Gmail, Meet)
- Slack or WhatsApp for internal comms
- Maybe Notion for personal notes or one team
- Excel/Sheets for tracking nearly everything else

### Their frustrations (in their words)
- "I'm always the one people come to for answers that are already documented."
- "We have a Google Drive with everything in it but nobody can find anything."
- "Every time we hire someone new, it's 2 weeks of hand-holding."
- "I can't be in every meeting but I need people to make the right calls."

### Their buying behaviour
- Discovers tools through peer recommendations (LinkedIn, WhatsApp groups, founder circles)
- Makes the call themselves for tools under ~$200/month — doesn't need procurement
- Needs to see it work in a demo before committing
- Will pilot with 5–10 people before rolling out
- Churns if setup takes more than a day

### Signal they're ready to buy
- Company crossed 15 people in the last 6 months
- Just onboarded someone who "really struggled"
- Recently had a mistake caused by someone acting on outdated info
- Founders are drowning in internal questions as the company grows

## Secondary ICP: The HR / People Lead

### Who they are
**Title:** HR Manager, People Operations Lead, Head of People, Talent Manager
**Company:** 30–150 people
**Focus:** Onboarding, policy, performance, compliance

### Their specific pain
They own the documentation — leave policies, onboarding checklists, contracts, HR FAQs. They wrote it all. Nobody reads it. They get DMs daily from people asking questions the docs already answer.

### What they want Kuzana to do
- Give new hires instant access to HR policies without involving the HR team
- Reduce DM volume for common questions
- Surface who to contact when a question falls outside the docs (e.g., unusual leave request → route to Amara)

### Why they're valuable to us
HR leads are influential buyers in professional services firms. If Kuzana removes even 30 minutes of their day, they'll become advocates. They also own the Google Drive folder most likely to have clean, structured documentation.

## Tertiary ICP: The Founder as First Customer

### Who they are
**Title:** Founder, CEO, Co-founder
**Stage:** Seed to Series A
**Company:** 10–40 people

### Their situation
They built the company by being in every room. Now they can't be. Knowledge that lived in their head hasn't been documented yet, or was documented once and is now out of date. New hires interrupt them constantly.

### What they want
Not a knowledge base — they don't have time to build one. They want something that works on what they already have, immediately, so they can get out of the loop.

### Why they're a good first customer
They make fast decisions. They have a disproportionate influence on tool adoption (if the founder uses it, the team uses it). They'll give honest product feedback. They're often connected to other founders who have the same problem.

## Who Is NOT Our ICP

| Profile | Why not |
|---|---|
| Company with <8 people | The knowledge problem isn't acute enough yet — everyone knows everyone |
| Company with >300 people | Likely already on Confluence or SharePoint; needs enterprise integrations we don't have |
| Microsoft 365 shops | Our connector is Google Workspace — Drive/Docs only for MVP |
| Companies with no documentation | Need a documentation culture first; Kuzana can't answer from docs that don't exist |
| Consumer / B2C buyers | This is a team tool, not a personal assistant |
| US/EU first priority | We're Africa-first; Western buyers are not the focus at this stage |

## ICP Persona: Meet Adaeze

**Adaeze Nwosu**, 34, Head of Operations at a 45-person fintech in Lagos.

She's been at the company for 3 years, joined when it was 12 people. She's seen the team triple and the chaos that comes with it. She manages everything that doesn't belong to Engineering or Finance — which means she manages almost everything.

Every Monday she gets 8–12 Slack messages asking things she's answered before. Leave requests. Expense process. Who handles client escalations. She's tried writing better docs. She's tried onboarding checklists. People still come to her.

She's not looking for a "knowledge management system." She's looking for the thing that stops her phone from buzzing on Monday mornings.

When she sees Kuzana return the exact leave policy chunk with a source link — and then route "who handles procurement?" to David Kariuki with his email — she gets it in 30 seconds. She doesn't need a 45-minute demo.

She'll pilot it with her team this week. If it works, she'll tell three other ops leads she knows in Nairobi and Accra.

**That is our buyer. Build for Adaeze.**
